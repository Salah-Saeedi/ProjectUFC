BUSINESS MODEL

The UFC is a global sports entertainment brand built on high-impact live events, star fighters, and premium media content. Its business model generates revenue through pay-per-view, broadcast partnerships, live events, sponsorships, and merchandise, while powerful fighter personalities fuel fan engagement. UFC’s audience is primarily young, digital-first fans aged 18–44, with a rapidly growing global following across the U.S., Brazil, the UK, Asia, and the Middle East, driving strong loyalty and repeat viewership.

UFC 229

Khabib vs McGregor

2.4 million PPV buys — the highest in UFC history.

UFC 229 stands as the most watched event in UFC history, generating approximately 2.4 million pay-per-view buys. The clash between undefeated champion Khabib Nurmagomedov and global superstar Conor McGregor transcended sport, becoming a cultural moment that attracted worldwide media attention and record-breaking revenue.

Diaz vs McGregor 2

Poirier vs McGregor 2

CORE STRENGTHS

Key advantages driving UFC's dominance in global combat sports

KEY LIMITATIONS

Challenges and constraints impacting UFC's long-term growth

Fighter Dependency

Heavy reliance on star fighters creates revenue risk when injuries, retirements, or controversies occur.

Regulatory Barriers

Different athletic commissions and regulations across countries slow global expansion.

Revenue Concentration

Pay-per-view remains a major income source, making earnings sensitive to event performance.

Fighter Pay Criticism

Ongoing debate over fighter compensation affects public perception and athlete relations.
